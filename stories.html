The Crow and the Pebbles

Once upon a time, a thirsty crow was flying around looking for water.

It was a hot day, and the crow was very tired.

Finally, the crow saw a pot with a little bit of water at the bottom.

It tried to drink from the pot, but the water was too low.

The crow couldn't reach it!

The clever crow thought of a plan. It picked up small pebbles

one by one and dropped them into the pot.

Slowly, the water in the pot began to rise.

After a few more pebbles, the water was high enough

for the crow to drink. The crow was happy and drank the cool water.

The crow smiled and flew away, feeling proud of its smart idea.

The Rabbit and the Tortoise

Once upon a time, there was a rabbit

who was very proud of how fast he could run.

One day, he challenged the tortoise to a race.

The tortoise was slow, but he accepted the challenge.

The race started, and the rabbit quickly ran far ahead.

Feeling confident, the rabbit decided to take a nap.

He thought, "I’m so fast, I’ll easily win the race!"

The tortoise kept going, slowly but surely.

While the rabbit slept, the tortoise passed him

and reached the finish line.

When the rabbit woke up, he saw the tortoise

had already won! The rabbit learned

that slow and steady wins the race.

The Lion and the Mouse

Once upon a time, there was a mighty lion who was taking a nap

in the forest. Suddenly, a little mouse ran across the lion's

paw, waking him up! waking him up! and caught the mouse

and caught the mouse in his large paw. The terrified

mouse begged the lion to let him go. "Please let me go,

and one day I will help you in return!" said the mouse.

The lion, feeling generous, decided to release the mouse.

A few days later, the lion was caught in a hunter's net

and roared in frustration. The mouse heard the lion's

cries and rushed to help. The mouse gnawed through

the ropes of the net and set the lion free. The lion was

so grateful and realized that even the smallest creatures

can be of great help. From that day on, the

lion and the mouse became good friends.

The Ant and the Grasshopper

Once, an ant worked hard all summer,

storing food for winter. A grasshopper asked,

"Why are you working so hard? Come, enjoy the sun!"

The ant replied, "I need food for the winter."

"Why are you working so hard? Come, enjoy the sun!"

When winter came, the grasshopper had no food and

asked the ant for help. The ant said,

"I worked hard, now I have enough food.

You should have planned ahead."

The grasshopper learned that hard work and

planning are important for the future
